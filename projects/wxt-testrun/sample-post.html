Digital Government “What We Heard” report

Beginning the Conversation…
A Made-in-Canada Approach to Digital Government

Executive summary

1-2 pages

Top-level overview of impetus for consultation, where visited and who talked to, and major findings from the consultation.

Why Digital Government?

3-4 pages

Overview of what is Digital Government, major trends, brief snapshot of what other jurisdictions have been doing (UK, US, Auz, NZ, Estonia), current landscape in Canada, and reasons for engaging in digital government consultation.

Starting a Conversation on a Made-in-Canada Approach

2 pages

Information on the consultation process, where was visited and who talked to (20 cities and 10 provinces/territories; over 1000 individuals representing more than 200 orgs), and what the themes were for the consultation (Organizing to Deliver Digital; Digital Talent; Digital Service Design and Delivery; Putting in Place the Right Foundations).

Organizing to Deliver Digital

3-4 pages

Short overview of what is meant by the topic and focus areas for the consultation discussions (Leadership structures for digital transformation; Digital operations management; Governance and coordination with other jurisdictions). Exploration of the key themes that came out of the consultations, highlighted with key quotes and/or examples from other jurisdictions:

  • Need for strong, central leadership to drive digital transformation
  • Finding the right balance between revolution and evolution
  • Evolving role of the Chief Information Officer and the emergence of the Chief Digital Officer function
  • Working in the open to accelerate change across jurisdictions
  • Critical role that data plays in improving service delivery, and the complex policy and perception issues around its use between organizations
  • The need to both maintain mission-critical legacy systems while being able to experiment and prototype new solutions in an agile way 

Digital Talent

3-4 pages

Short overview of what is meant by the topic and focus areas for the consultation discussion (Recruitment and retention of employees with digital skills; Building digital capacity through training;). Exploration of the key themes that came out of the consultations, highlighted with key quotes and/or examples from other jurisdictions:

  • Government is a black-box when it comes to recruitment from the outside - need to find better ways to connect external talent and internal opportunities
  • Many in tech sector are mission-driven - not looking for 30yr career but want impact - short-term tours of duty, flexibility
  • Engage with design and digital talent in universities differently and earlier - GC approach is far behind competitors in the tech sector going for the same talent pool
  • There is an emerging “third-way” between simply build or buy - co-creation opportunities
  • Need to work in different ways - not just have to come to Ottawa / innovation outposts, co-creation, partnering with employees of private sector firms,
  • How do we leverage the talent we have in government already, enable innovation from within, strengthen it with external talent, help to integrate external talent

Digital Service Design and Delivery

3-4 pages

Short overview of what is meant by the topic and focus areas for the consultation discussions (Delivering experimental prototypes and pilot projects; Agile, iterative, and scalable approaches to development; User-centred improvement and customization of digital solutions; User research, design thinking, and usability). Exploration of the key themes that came out of the consultations, highlighted with key quotes and/or examples from other jurisdictions:

  • User needs to be at the centre of all service design - lots of implications of this
  • Iterative approaches are critical to success, but hard to fit into existing govt processes
  • Digital Design standards can drive change across the system, but they also need to be tailored to the Canadian context: Accessibility, low-bandwidth access (rural & north), Official Languages
  • The sweet spot between design thinking and digital - ripe area to explore
  • Need for someone to play a central role in scaling innovation, connecting the dots 

Putting in Place the Right Foundations

3-4 pages

Short overview of what is meant by the topic and focus areas for the consultation discussions (Digital infrastructure; Enterprise digital identity and standards; Procurement modernization). Exploration of the key themes that came out of the consultations, highlighted with key quotes and/or examples from other jurisdictions:

  • Procurement continues to be a challenge - how do we bite of small, agile pieces?
  • Cloud and open source become key building blocks to digital innovation
  • Digital Identity is key building block needed to enable better digital service across boundaries
  • Broader digital literacy needed within government to ensure better decision-making 

Continuing the Conversation

2 pages

Positioning of this consultation as the beginning of an ongoing conversation to build a Made-in-Canada approach to digital government that will require sharing experiences and learnings from all levels of government and with private, academic, and community sector partners. Provide options to keep in touch with the team (email, Twitter handles, etc.); include a moderated comment functionality on the site to allow for readers to post feedback and discuss the report on the site (similar to what is currently available on the open.canada.ca site).
